Lumpia

Description

Lumpia is a spring roll-like dish that originated in China, Indonesia, and the Philippines. It's made with a thin, paper-like wrapper and a savory or sweet filling, and is often served as an appetizer or snack.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds ground pork, beef, or chicken
  • 1 large potato, diced
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 1 cup green beans. diced
  • 1 large green bell pepper, diced
  • 1 bag (10-oz) sliced carrots
  • 1 bag rice noodles
  • 6 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can bean sprouts, drained
  • 50 lumpia wrappers
  • Dashida Korean beef stock
  • salt and pepper

Steps

  1. Brown the ground meat with the diced onions and garlic.
  2. Add 4 tablespoons Dashida seasoning.
  3. Add the beans.
  4. Add the carrots.
  5. Add the potatoes.
  6. Add the bell peppers.
  7. Soak the noodles in hot water until softened. use kitchen shears to cut into small pieces.
  8. Add the noodles to the pot.
  9. Add bean sprouts.
  10. Stir; cook until potatoes are almost done, but still firm. Taste and adjust seasonings.
  11. Place lumpia wrapper on a flat surface. Place 2-3 table spoons of filling on one edge. Tightly fold the edge over the filling. Fold side towards the middle. Continue to tightly roll the lumpia toward the opposite edge. Stop rolling about an inch away from the edge. Moisten the edge with water, or a mixture of water and flour. Roll the lumpia to the edge, pressing gently tp seal. Fry in vegatable oil (peanut oil works great too) until goldrn brown.
